Report: Generation of Simulated Options Data

Objective

The purpose of this simulation was to create a dataset of options prices with realistic variations in implied volatility (IV) and strike prices, suitable for use in evaluating the Crank-Nicholson method and comparing it with high-frequency trading (HFT) strategies.

Data Generation Process

  1. Stock Price:

    • The underlying stock price was set at $100 to provide a reference for option pricing.

  2. Strike Prices:

    • Strike prices were chosen in a range of $90 to $110, ensuring the dataset included:
      • In-the-money options: Strike price below $100 (for calls).
      • At-the-money options: Strike price close to $100.
      • Out-of-the-money options: Strike price above $100 (for calls).

  3. Time to Maturity:

    • Time to maturity was simulated from 0.01 years to 1 year, providing a mix of short-term and long-term options.

  4. Implied Volatility (IV):

    • A base IV of 20% (0.2) was used, with random variations applied to simulate realistic market conditions.
    • Random normal noise (μ=0,σ=0.05μ=0,σ=0.05) was added, with IVs clamped between **10% and 40%**for practical scenarios.

  5. Option Prices:

    • Option prices were calculated using the Black-Scholes model, a widely used analytical formula for pricing European-style options.
    • Both call prices and put prices were computed for each combination of strike price, maturity, and IV.
    • Prices were rounded to two decimal places for readability.

  6. Final Dataset:

    • The dataset includes 100 options with columns for:
      • Stock Price.
      • Strike Price.
      • Time to Maturity (in years).
      • Call Price.
      • Put Price.
      • Implied Volatility (variable).

Use Cases

This dataset can be used for:

  1. Crank-Nicholson Method:
    • To calculate implied volatility (IV) and compare it with pre-simulated values.
  2. HFT Strategy Testing:
    • Analyzing pricing models for real-time trend determination.
  3. Educational Purposes:
    • Demonstrating the relationship between IV, strike prices, and option prices.
